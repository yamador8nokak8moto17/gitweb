Timetable for 6155 Projects

(last updated: 19 January 1999)

A completed project consists of two things. The first is a paper of about 10 pages typeset, which discusses the mathematics you've learned. It should include references, definitions, statements of theorems, motivation, lots of examples, and proofs where appropriate. Try to assimilate what you read in different sources, and generate your own examples. The paper need not be original research, but it should be your own work when you're done. In mathematics, it's not necessary to footnote every detail, but your bibliography should include every source you use, as well as some you might have read and not used.

The second is a 50-minute presentation to the other members of the class. This will probably overlap some with your paper, but oral presentation is a different skill from written. Choosing good examples and motivating the definitions and theorems is even more important here. You won't have time to prove everything, but you need to at least "induce a benevolent feeling of credulity" (in the words of Paul Halmos) in the audience.

Please accomplish the following task by the date listed. I will try to give you lots of helpful feedback to make your presentations and papers excellent. Please ask me for help whenever you need it.

Grading: The primary criterion will be the clarity of the exposition. The paper and the presentation count equally. Please proofread carefully, since errors in punctuation and grammaer, ambiguous statements, and incorrect formulas can be very confusing.
