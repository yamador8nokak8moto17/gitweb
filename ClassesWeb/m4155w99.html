Quizzes: There will be a short quiz at the end of class each Tuesday on the assigned reading. I will try to be very specific about what you should know. If you're not sure, please ask!

Final Projects: Instead of a final exam, 4155 you may elect to work on a project which involves researching some topic in the combinatorics literature, writing a paper, and presenting to the instructor. Such a project, including a presentation to the class, will be required of everyone taking the course for graduate credit (6155). Topics must be choosen and approved by the instructor by Thursday 1/21. Start thinking *now* about what might interest you. The skills you exercise for this project will be extremely useful to you in the future, even if you don't use the particular mathematics you study ever again. Midterm Exam: Tuesday February 16 in class. Please rearrange your schedule now if necessary.

Final Exam: Tuesday March 16 2:00--3:50.

Content: Combinatorics is the art of counting. We will study many different ways to count things, and use tools from calculus, linear algebra, and other parts of mathematics. There are many beautiful results with elegant proofs, which require very little background to understand. Combinatorics has relatively recently come into its own as an accepted mainstream area of research, but combinatorial problems have been intriguing mathematicians in most fields for centuries. The only way to learn mathematics is by doing it! Complete each assignment to the best of your ability, and get help when you are confused. Come to class prepared with questions, having read the text with pencil and paper at hand. Don't hesitate to seek help from other students. Sometimes the point of view of someone who has just figured something out can be the most helpful.
