Research

My research is in algebraic combinatorics, specifically in dynamical algebraic combinatorics, where we consider actions on combinatorial objects, their orbit structures, and how these orbits related to statistics on the objects. James Propp and I coined the term homomesy (Greek for "same middle") to describe the phenomenon where a statistics has the same average over every orbit.

I'm also interested in math educational outreach to K-16 students and teachers, and have worked with many programs to increase engagement and understanding in various levels.

See my research page for more.
